Welcome!

I'm Alessa Cross, a third-year at the University of Chicago pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science. I am Japanese and Australian.

I first started programming in October 2017. While I've just started my journey in computing, I have worked on several projects and taught myself a few languages so feel free to take a look at what I've done so far.

I am intrigued by how technology can be used to make up for where humans are unwilling or unable to change for the better. Specifically, in regards to social issues that affect women and gender minorities, change can be very slow -- too slow. Luckily, technology changes fast and I believe there are so many ways new innovations can mitigate the damage and negative experiences we face daily. In particular I would love to employ Machine Learning and Object Recognition to create a safer online environment for women. Last year, my goal was to learn how to code -- this year, I want to immerse myself in those two specific fields.

I am excited to continue adding to this page and to build cooler projects with more beautifully organized code with each passing day.
